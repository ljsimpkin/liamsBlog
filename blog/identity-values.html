Who am I and how has my culture influenced me?

21 Apr 2022

Kia ora everyone! I am Liam, I am from NZ and I grew up in Auckland. After doing my undergraduate in business at Otago I knew I wanted to pursue a career in the technology space. This took me on an adventure to Silicon Valley where I stumbled upon software engineering. Since then I have worked at a beekeeping startup, a software studio, and now I'm taking my tech skills to the next level at Dev Academy!

I am excited to build cutting edge technology. Some of humanity's greatest achievements have occurred through this and software engineering plays a large part in building these tools today. The constantly changing environment, ability to bring your ideas to life, and limitless growth potential, is hugely exciting to me.

My culture and its influence on my values and identity.

There are 3 cultures I feel strongly make up my values and identity. Firstly, the New Zealand culture, secondly my family culture, and thirdly human culture more broadly.

For me, being a kiwi is about being easy going. We don’t tend to make up problems. We go with the flow and be kind to others along the way. We love the outdoors and have an awesome ability to build things ingenuitively. We value family and friends and leave our front door open for anybody with good intentions to come in. As a result I have found it quite easy to interact with other cultures, avoid issues, and enjoy. Avoiding issues can also be bad if it’s done too often on things that don’t go away. I’ve learnt how useful and connecting these issues can be when they’re talked about in the right way.

In my home growing up, 2 values were strongly installed into me. From my mum, it is the value of positivity and from my dad, the value of doing something you love. My mum sharpened this idea her whole life and there’s something deeper to be understood about it. We all know positivity can improve our happiness. We think about an event and if it was good or bad it doesn’t matter, there is always some positive element that can be drawn from it. That’s straightforward. But having the mindset to look for positivity also helps us have more positive experiences. It’s not just the memory of those experiences that become positive in our minds, it’s the actual experiences in the moment too. We become better at deciding what experiences we want to choose because we are better at noticing positivity. When we have this frame of mind, the moment and the memory become more positive too. This has tremendously shaped my attitude which has helped in past jobs like door to door sales, which I actually really enjoyed (in the moment and upon reflection too haha). The other value my dad taught me is doing something you love. Our lives are short so lets make the most of it and enjoy every minute.

I am also a human, and I think as a human we have an innate sense of empathy and fairness. I want a world that is fair, where people have access to equal opportunities. A world with unlimited potential for creation. A world full of liberty but also compassion for helping others. I believe humans have the right to be free to make their own choices but this sometimes comes into conflict when someone’s freedom impacts our own, like with vaccines. It’s so interesting when our values clash, I guess they don’t define us perfectly. There’s weights to them. Spectrums and contradictions.
